Skin Lesion

A skin lesion is an abnormal change in the skin that can be benign or malignant. In this project, we focus on 7 classes of lesions:

  • bkl: Benign Keratosis-like Lesions – Rough, scaly patches often seen in older individuals.
  • nv: Melanocytic Nevi – Common moles that are usually benign.
  • df: Dermatofibroma – Firm nodules that are typically benign.
  • mel: Melanoma – A malignant tumor of melanocytes, very dangerous if not treated.
  • vasc: Vascular Lesions – Lesions that arise from blood vessels, typically benign.
  • bcc: Basal Cell Carcinoma – A common type of skin cancer, usually localized.
  • akiec: Actinic Keratoses – Rough, scaly patches that may progress to cancer if untreated.

Classification

Classification involves using a Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) to analyze images and assign them to one of the 7 skin lesion categories. The CNN model used in this project has been evaluated using performance metrics such as accuracy, loss curves, and a confusion matrix.


Segmentation

Segmentation involves using a UNet model to delineate the area of the skin lesion from the background. The UNet model’s performance has been evaluated using segmentation accuracy metrics and a confusion matrix for the pixel-wise predictions.
